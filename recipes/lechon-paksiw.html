Lechon Paksiw

If you have ever gone to a Filipino party and brought some lechon home, this lechon paksiw recipe might be for you. Lechon paksiw is another way of cooking the Lechon. It is usually a soup filled with leftover Lechon and other ingridients such as carrots, sayote, and pechay as vegetables. This soup is seasoned to the taste and gives of a satisfying flavor that fills your stomach to the brim.

Ingridients

  • Leftover Lechon
  • Your allions such as garlic and onions
  • Vegetable of Choice
  • Salt, Pepper, and possibly Sugar
  • A bit of Soy Sauce

Equipment

  • Knife
  • Cooking Pot
  • Laddle

Steps

  1. Grab your leftover Lechon and slice it to bits.
  2. Also chop your vegetables and alliums.
  3. Boil water depending on the amount of soup you want.
  4. If the water is already boiling, add your sliced Lechon bits and lower the fire to slowly cook it.
  5. Add your alliums to the pot as this will enhance the flavor.
  6. Add your seasonings according to your liking. Take note that your Lechon might be already salty so use your seasonings in moderation
  7. Add your vegetables now and slowly cook, stop around 5 minutes after the vegetables have been added so that they wouldn't be overcooked.
  8. Serve in a bowl and you're ready to go!
